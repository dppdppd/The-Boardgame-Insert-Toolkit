  2. **Slope angle:** initial fix used radial inset (`r - c`), giving steeper-than-45° slopes on polygons. Corrected to inset the apothem (`r - c/cos(180°/n)`) so each face slopes exactly 45° perpendicular to itself.

Bottom ring's outer cylinder shared an exact face with the cavity wall, and inner cone tapered to zero thickness at z=c. Fattened outer by `oeps=0.01` (overlaps wall material instead of coinciding) and overshot inner cone past outer at the top (ring closes with finite thickness). `Simple: yes` confirmed by CGAL on the 5-box `test_chamfer_n` test.

## Key Discoveries
- `PositionInnerLayer` (line 1789) translates compartment-frame `z=0` by `m_wall_thickness`, so the cavity fill (`__smallest_partition_height + m_component_base_height`) reaches exactly the box top — that's why hex/oct/round outlines appear at the box top despite the carve cube being taller than the fill cube.
- Non-square cavity geometry is built as `cube_carve - (cube_addback - MakeCompartmentShape)`, so the wall material between the polygon and its bounding cube is added back in the additions stage. Chamfers placed at bounding-box edges sit inside this added material and have zero visible effect.
- For polygonal chamfers, perpendicular wall offset ≠ radial inset; concentric polygons of radius `r` and `r-d` have perpendicular wall-to-wall distance `d·cos(180°/n)`.
- A single non-manifold cavity exports without warning; the issue compounds when several are unioned, presumably because shared/coincident faces accumulate enough degeneracy to trip CGAL.

The eventual manifold fix made the post-process slice work too, but it's still fragile because OpenSCAD's `import(stl)` can fail to reconstruct a CGAL polyhedron from STL even on simple inputs.
